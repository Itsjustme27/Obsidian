**Back Pain Relief: Stretching Routine for Study Sessions**

### **1. Cat-Cow Stretch**

**Purpose**: Improves flexibility and relieves tension in the spine.

1. Get on your hands and knees.
2. Inhale, arch your back (cow pose), letting your belly drop and lifting your head.
3. Exhale, round your back upward (cat pose), tucking your chin into your chest.
4. Repeat slowly for 8-10 cycles.

### **2. Child’s Pose (Yoga Stretch)**

**Purpose**: Stretches your lower back and hips.

1. Kneel on the floor with your knees apart.
2. Extend your arms forward and lower your torso to the floor.
3. Rest your forehead on the floor and relax.
4. Hold for 20-30 seconds, breathing deeply.
![[Pasted image 20241218184500.jpg]]
---

### **3. Seated Spinal Twist**

**Purpose**: Relieves tension in the back and promotes spinal mobility.

1. Sit on a chair with your feet flat on the floor.
2. Place your right hand on the backrest and your left hand on your right knee.
3. Twist your torso to the right, looking over your shoulder.
4. Hold for 20 seconds, then switch sides.
![[Pasted image 20241218184550.jpg]]
---

### **4. Standing Forward Fold**

**Purpose**: Stretches the hamstrings, lower back, and spine.

1. Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart.
2. Bend forward at the hips and let your arms and head hang down.
3. Hold for 20-30 seconds, keeping a slight bend in your knees if necessary.
![[Pasted image 20241218184606.jpg]]
---

### **5. Glute Bridge**

**Purpose**: Strengthens your lower back and glutes.

1. Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor.
2. Lift your hips off the ground until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees.
3. Squeeze your glutes at the top, then lower back down slowly.
4. Perform 10-15 reps.
![[Pasted image 20241218184624.jpg]]
---

### **6. Wall Stretch**

**Purpose**: Stretches the upper back and shoulders.

1. Stand facing a wall.
2. Place both hands on the wall at shoulder height.
3. Step back slightly and let your chest sink toward the floor.
4. Hold for 20-30 seconds.
![[Pasted image 20241218184649.jpg]]
---

### **7. Reclining Spinal Twist**

**Purpose**: Relaxes your lower back and stretches your spine.

1. Lie on your back with your arms stretched out to the sides.
2. Bend your knees and let them drop to one side while keeping your upper body flat.
3. Hold for 20 seconds, then switch sides.
![[Pasted image 20241218184706.jpg]]
---

### **8. Hip Flexor Stretch**

**Purpose**: Loosens tight hip flexors, which often contribute to back pain.

1. Kneel on one knee with the other leg in front (like a lunge position).
2. Push your hips slightly forward until you feel a stretch in the front of your hip.
3. Hold for 20 seconds, then switch sides.
![[Pasted image 20241218184740.jpg]]
---

### **Quick Routine Summary**

1. **Cat-Cow Stretch**: 1 minute
2. **Child’s Pose**: 30 seconds
3. **Seated Spinal Twist**: 40 seconds (20 seconds each side)
4. **Standing Forward Fold**: 30 seconds
5. **Glute Bridge**: 10-15 reps
6. **Wall Stretch**: 30 seconds
7. **Reclining Spinal Twist**: 40 seconds (20 seconds each side)
8. **Hip Flexor Stretch**: 40 seconds (20 seconds each side)

---

### **Tips for Success**

- Perform these stretches every 1-2 hours during long study sessions.
- Breathe deeply while stretching to maximize relaxation.
- Stay consistent to see long-term benefits.
